About Me

Subhranil Dutta

Post-Doctoral Fellow

Room No: B64 - 310, Institute of Computer Science, University of St. Gallen, Torstrasse 25, CH-9000 St. Gallen, Switzerland

I am currently serving as a Post-Doctoral Fellow in the Institute of Computer Science at the University of St Gallen, Switzerland, under the guidance of Professor Katerina Mitrokotsa with the Swiss Government Excellence Scholarship (ESKAS). Prior to this, I held the position of Research Assistant in the Institute of Computer Science at University of St Gallen, Switzerland. My academic journey includes completing my doctoral research as a senior research scholar in the Department of Mathematics at Indian Institute of Technology Kharagpur, India where I defended my thesis under the supervision of Professor Sourav Mukhopadhyay. My academic foundation includes a B.Sc. from Malda College, University of Gour Banga, and an M.Sc. in Pure Mathematics from the University of Gour Banga.

Latest News

  • October 2024: Presented a talk at the Annual Quantum Computing Conference in Geneva.
  • September 2024: Awarded the Best Post-Doctoral Research Award at the University of St Gallen.

Research Interests

My primary research interests include:

  • Functional Encryption: Developing efficient and secure encryption schemes for practical applications.
  • Lattice-Based Cryptography: Designing cryptographic primitives that are resistant to quantum computing attacks.
  • Post-Quantum Cryptography: Exploring innovative methods to enhance security in a post-quantum world.
  • Privacy-Preserving Protocols: Focusing on protocols that ensure data privacy and security.
